Lion

Lions live for about 10-14 years in the wild. While in captivity they live for as long as 20-25 years old.

  • Habitat: Sub-Saharan Africa
  • Hunting: Medium to large-sized hoofed animals such as wildebeests, zebras, and antelopes
  • Diet: An adult lioness requires an average of about 5 kg (11 lb) of meat per day while males require about 7 kg (15 lb)
  • Reproduction: Most lionesses reproduce by the time they are four years of age

The lion (Panthera leo) is a species in the family Felidae; it is a muscular, deep-chested cat with a short, rounded head, a reduced neck and round ears, and a hairy tuft at the end of its tail. It is sexually dimorphic; male lions have a prominent mane, which is the most recognisable feature of the species.
